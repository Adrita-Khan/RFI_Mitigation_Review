    <h2>Abstract</h2>

    <p>Radio Frequency Interference (RFI) poses significant challenges to radio astronomy by contaminating observational data, thereby affecting the quality and reliability of astronomical measurements. Traditional RFI mitigation techniques, while effective to a certain extent, often struggle with the increasing complexity and volume of data generated by modern radio telescopes. This repository presents a comprehensive review of RFI mitigation strategies in radio astronomy, emphasizing the application of Machine Learning (ML) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) methodologies. Through an extensive literature survey, comparative analyses, and practical implementations, this work aims to highlight the advancements, benefits, and potential of ML and AI in enhancing RFI mitigation processes.</p>

    <h2 id="introduction">Introduction</h2>

    <p>Radio Frequency Interference (RFI) is an unwanted signal that disrupts the data collection processes of radio telescopes. As radio astronomy advances, the proliferation of electronic devices and communication systems exacerbates the RFI problem, necessitating more sophisticated mitigation techniques. This review explores the integration of Machine Learning (ML) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) in developing effective RFI mitigation strategies, assessing their performance against traditional methods.</p>

    <h2 id="background">Background</h2>

    <h3 id="radio-astronomy">Radio Astronomy</h3>

    <p>Radio astronomy involves the study of celestial objects through their radio frequency emissions. It provides critical insights into phenomena such as pulsars, quasars, and cosmic microwave background radiation. However, the sensitivity of radio telescopes makes them particularly susceptible to RFI, which can obscure or mimic astronomical signals.</p>

    <h3 id="rfi-overview">RFI Overview</h3>

    <p>RFI originates from various terrestrial and extraterrestrial sources, including satellites, mobile phones, broadcasting stations, and even natural sources like lightning. The impact of RFI on radio astronomical observations includes data corruption, increased noise levels, and false detections, all of which compromise the integrity of scientific results.</p>

    <h2 id="literature-review">Literature Review</h2>

    <h3 id="traditional-mitigation-techniques">Traditional Mitigation Techniques</h3>

    <p>Traditional RFI mitigation approaches encompass hardware-based solutions like shielding and filtering, as well as software-based methods such as thresholding and time-frequency analysis. While these techniques have been instrumental in managing RFI, they often fall short in dynamically adapting to the evolving RFI landscape.</p>

    <h3 id="machine-learning-and-ai-methods">Machine Learning and AI Methods</h3>

    <p>Recent advancements in ML and AI offer promising alternatives for RFI mitigation. Techniques such as supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and deep learning have been employed to identify and classify RFI, enabling more adaptive and intelligent mitigation strategies. These methods leverage large datasets and computational power to enhance detection accuracy and reduce false positives.</p>

    <h3 id="comparative-analysis">Comparative Analysis</h3>

    <p>This section juxtaposes traditional and ML/AI-based RFI mitigation techniques, evaluating their effectiveness, scalability, and adaptability. The analysis highlights the strengths of ML and AI approaches in handling complex and high-dimensional data, as well as their limitations in terms of computational requirements and the need for labeled datasets.</p>
